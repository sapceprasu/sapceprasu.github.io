About

I am a researcher focused on making language models more interpretable, psychologically grounded, and behaviourally controllable. My broader goal is to connect modern NLP with ideas from psychology, human values, and real-world interaction design.

I am currently a PhD candidate at the University of Western Australia, where I work on research spanning LLM personality assessment, persona consistency in discourse, activation engineering, and sequential preference alignment.

My published and ongoing work asks questions such as: how stable are personality traits in large language models, how can we evaluate them using established psychological instruments, can LLM agents maintain personas across multi-turn discourse, and what happens when we try to steer model behaviour directly in activation space.

Before starting my PhD on 1 March 2024, I built experience across computer engineering, data science, software development, teaching, and research supervision. I have also worked in frontend development and led teams, which continues to shape the practical and product-facing side of how I think about AI systems.

My long-term interest is to develop LLMs that better understand and respond to the emotional, cognitive, and behavioural dimensions of human interaction, while remaining rigorous, safe, and useful in real settings.

Research

My work combines psychological theory, empirical evaluation, and model intervention methods to better understand how language models behave and how those behaviours can be measured and shaped.

2025

Evaluating Personality Traits in Large Language Models: Insights from Psychological Questionnaires

Poster presented at The Web Conference 2025 in Sydney. This work studies LLM personality traits using established psychological questionnaires, examines consistency and dimensional dominance, and compares OpenAI and Llama models.

Presented
2025

Can LLM Agents Maintain a Persona in Discourse?

Research on personality alignment and consistency in multi-turn, agent-based discourse, focusing on whether persona-conditioned LLM agents can sustain coherent behavioural signals across conversations.

Research
2026

Activation Engineering for Big Five Personality Steering in Large Language Models

Accepted at EACL 2026 (main). This project studies trait-specific steering vectors in activation space, hybrid layer selection, and the trade-off between targeted control, fluency, and cross-trait behavioural interference.

Accepted
Ongoing

Factorized Temporal Drift in Preference Alignment

Current work on non-stationary preference learning and sequential DPO, investigating whether preference drift is anisotropic across factors such as harmlessness, helpfulness, and style, and how factor-aware optimization may improve robustness.

Ongoing

Selected Projects

Alongside publications, I build experimental pipelines, evaluation frameworks, and analysis tools around controllable LLM behaviour, psychometric testing, and fine-tuning.

P.01

Psychometric Evaluation of LLMs

A research pipeline for administering psychological questionnaires such as TIPI and BFI to language models, comparing trait distributions, consistency patterns, and behavioural biases across closed- and open-source systems.

Python LLM Evaluation Psychology Data Analysis
P.02

Persona Consistency in Agent Dialogue

An agent-based discourse evaluation framework for studying whether persona-conditioned models maintain stable high or low Big Five traits across topics, turns, and conversational settings.

Agents Dialogue Judge Models NLP
P.03

Activation Steering & Subspace Analysis

A modular activation extraction and intervention framework for studying shared personality subspaces, trait directions, SVD-based geometry, and inference-time behavioural control in models such as LLaMA and Mistral.

PyTorch Transformers Activation Hooks Interpretability
P.04

Sequential Preference Alignment Diagnostics

Experimental work on sequential DPO and related training dynamics, including reward margins, policy preference accuracy, flip analysis, gradient behaviour, and stage-wise evaluation across multiple alignment factors.

DPO LoRA / QLoRA TRL Diagnostics
P.05

Personality Trait Classification from Text

Fine-tuning and benchmarking models such as BERT, RoBERTa, Flan-T5, LLaMA, and Mistral for personality detection using essay and social text datasets, with emphasis on trait specificity, instruction tuning, and robust evaluation.

Fine-tuning Classification Hugging Face Datasets

Teaching, Mentoring, and Research Support

Academic support work involving tutorials, supervision assistance, experiment tracking, research documentation, and technical guidance for student projects and university coursework.
